NASA JPL & HeroX “Honey, I Shrunk the Payload” Challenge

This case competition, which I competed in as a part of a team of students from UT, challenged participants to design a very low resource and low volume module for a lunar lander. The idea was that this module would fill open space and power allocations on a future lander, to maximize the productivity of the lander. Teams were given a list of potential areas of scientific interest and lander pathing, then left to design their own solution. My contributions to the project were primarily early research and CAD.

Our team decided to focus on an absorption spectroscopy solution to sample lunar regolith across a number of sites, but specifically the lunar swirls Schrodinger Crater and Reiner Gamma. The module would have had a gas capture chamber that was lowered to the surface. Once sealed, the module would use a capacitor bank to store the small amount of power we were given until the laser could be fired to get a reading from the regolith.
