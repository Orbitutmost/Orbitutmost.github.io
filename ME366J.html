This project-based class led groups through the entire process of product design, from interest studies through a finished round of prototyping. While ideation and concept generation were whole-team efforts, each member of our team had a more specialized role in the prototyping process. My primary roles were software/electronics and build, although I also contributed to design and fabrication efforts.

My software role on this project involved diving into and implementing the GRBL open source CNC software on an arduino with motor shield, setting up the software pipeline to get from a vector drawing to gcode, and running that gcode on the project. GRBL is a highly optimized software package, getting the absolute most it can out of the limited computing resources of an arduino. As a result, any customization needs to take place in the gcode file; GRBL is compressed and fairly difficult to edit for a novice. To this end, I wrote a small python script to convert a CSV into strings of gcode, allowing me to fine tune the output for the project. For example, the Z axis scissor lift was driven by a stepper motor attached to a drive screw. This resulted in a non-linear relationship between turns of the stepper and difference in height depending on where in the extension of the scissor lift you were. This was taken into account during gcode production, to produce the intended output. The gcode was then run on the machine via the Universal Gcode Sender and Candle modules.
